A. P. J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam , fondly remembered as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist and a beloved President who left an indelible mark on India's space and defense programs. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, his journey from a humble background to becoming an aerospace engineer and a key architect of India's missile technology is inspiring. Renowned for his pivotal role in developing the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles, his contributions transcended the scientific community. As the 11th President of India, he became the "People's President," admired for his humility and dedication to education. His legacy endures through his writings and the countless lives he inspired to dream big and strive for excellence.

#Biography

  • Early Life : Born into a modest family, Kalam excelled in academics, particularly in mathematics.
  • Education : He earned a degree in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later pursued Aeronautical Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Career : Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was instrumental in the development of the SLV-III and the successful orbiting of the Rohini satellite in 1980.
  • Missile Programs : He headed the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP), leading to the creation of missiles like AGNI and PRITHVI.
  • Presidency : Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was widely respected and known as the "People's President" for his dedication to education and inspiring the youth2.
  • Legacy : Post-presidency, he continued to engage with the public through lectures and writing, authoring several books, including his autobiography, Wings of Fire
